Critical Thinking

Logic, Reasoning, and Decision Making
Problem solving involves understanding a situation, considering solutions, and choosing the most effective one.

Developing problem-solving skills helps individuals become more independent, confident, and successful in various situations.

Lectures

1. Analysis

Analysis is breaking down complex information into smaller parts to understand it better. It involves identifying key points, patterns, and connections to make informed decisions.

2. Evaluation

Evaluation is judging the credibility and relevance of information and arguments. It requires careful assessment of sources, logic, and evidence to make sound decisions.

3. Inference

Inference is drawing logical conclusions from available evidence, even when all details are not stated. It helps in predicting outcomes and understanding hidden meanings.

4. Explanation

Explanation is clearly communicating reasoning and conclusions. It organizes ideas with supporting evidence to help others understand your thought process.

5. Self-Regulation

Self-regulation is managing one’s own thinking, recognizing biases, and adjusting strategies to improve problem-solving. It promotes disciplined, objective, and critical thinking.
