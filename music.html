Music Theory

Reading Music
It teaches the basics of melody, harmony, rhythm, and tempo, which are important in creating and understanding music.

Music theory helps learners read musical notes and understand how songs are built.

Lectures

1. Form and Structure

Form refers to the overall type, genre, or external shape of a literary work (e.g., sonnet, novel, play). Structure refers to the internal organization, sequence of events, and arrangement of parts within that work (e.g., plot, stanza, rhyme scheme). Form is the "what" (classification), while structure is the "how" (internal mechanics).

2. Chords and Harmony

Harmony is when multiple notes harmonize. It's just different notes played together, that's all a harmony is. So a chord progression is essentially a sequence of harmonies. Play a C chord it could be considered a harmony. Play a C chord, then a G chord, then a D, then back to C, that's a chord progression.

3. Musical Analysis

Music Analysis involves dissecting and examining various elements within a musical composition to gain deeper insights into its structure, meaning, and emotional impact. By delving into these elements, analysts can uncover the intricate craftsmanship and intentions of the composer.

4. Music Genre

Although countless styles and subcultures have emerged, five primary music genres still define the framework of global music classification: Pop, Rock, Hip-Hop, Jazz, and Classical. Each has influenced the development of hundreds of subgenres and remains a cornerstone of musical creativity.

5.Performance Skills

Performance skills in music refer to the abilities needed to present music in front of an audience with confidence and expression. It includes proper singing or playing of instruments with good timing, rhythm, and pitch. A performer must also show stage presence, which means connecting with the audience through emotions and body language. Good performance skills require practice, focus, and control of nervousness during live presentations. It also involves teamwork when performing in groups or bands to create harmony and coordination. Overall, performance skills help a musician deliver music in a powerful, engaging, and meaningful way.
