Human Anatomy

Body Systems and Functions.
Human Anatomy is the study of the structure of the human body and how its parts are organized.

We learn how the body functions and how to take care of it properly.

Lectures

1. Skeletal System (bones and joints)

Skeletal System (Bones and Joints) is the part of human anatomy that provides the body with structure, support, and protection. It is made up of 206 bones in the adult human body, including the skull, spine, ribs, arms, and legs. Bones work together to support the body’s shape and protect important organs like the brain, heart, and lungs. Joints are the places where two or more bones meet, allowing movement and flexibility. There are different types of joints such as hinge joints (like knees and elbows) and ball-and-socket joints (like shoulders and hips). The skeletal system also works with muscles to help the body move and perform daily activities. Overall, it is essential for support, movement, and protection of vital organs.

2. Muscular System (muscles and movement)

Muscular System (Muscles and Movement) is the part of human anatomy that allows the body to move and perform physical activities. It is made up of more than 600 muscles that work together with the bones and joints. Muscles help us walk, run, write, smile, and even breathe. There are three main types of muscles: skeletal muscles (for movement of the body), smooth muscles (found in internal organs like the stomach), and cardiac muscle (found in the heart). Skeletal muscles are controlled by our will, while smooth and cardiac muscles work automatically.

3. Circulatory System (heart and blood flow)

Circulatory System (Heart and Blood Flow) is a major system in human anatomy responsible for transporting blood, oxygen, nutrients, and waste products throughout the body. Its main organ is the heart, which acts as a pump that keeps blood moving continuously. The system also includes blood vessels such as arteries, veins, and capillaries that carry blood to and from different parts of the body. Arteries carry oxygen-rich blood away from the heart, while veins bring oxygen-poor blood back to the heart. The circulatory system helps deliver oxygen and nutrients to cells so the body can function properly.

4. Respiratory System (lungs and breathing)

Respiratory System (Lungs and Breathing) is the system in the human body responsible for taking in oxygen and removing carbon dioxide. It mainly involves the lungs, nose, trachea (windpipe), and diaphragm. When we inhale, air enters through the nose or mouth and travels down to the lungs. In the lungs, oxygen from the air is exchanged with carbon dioxide in the blood through tiny air sacs called alveoli. When we exhale, carbon dioxide is released out of the body. The diaphragm helps control breathing by moving up and down to allow air in and out of the lungs.

5. Nervous System (brain, spinal cord, and nerves)

Nervous System (Brain, Spinal Cord, and Nerves) is the control and communication system of the human body. It is made up of the brain, spinal cord, and a network of nerves that spread throughout the body. The brain acts as the main control center, responsible for thinking, memory, emotions, and decision-making. The spinal cord carries messages between the brain and the rest of the body. The nerves send signals to and from different parts of the body, allowing us to feel sensations like pain, touch, and temperature. The nervous system helps control both voluntary actions (like walking and speaking) and involuntary actions (like breathing and heartbeat).
