Creative Writing

Storytelling, Essays, and Poetry
The art of expressing thoughts, ideas, and emotions imaginatively. It includes writing stories, poems, and other creative forms.

Creative writing allows exploration of imagination, development of unique ideas, and communication of feelings through words.

Lectures

1. Elements of a Story

Plot, setting, and characters form the backbone of storytelling. A good plot engages readers, a setting establishes atmosphere, and characters bring the story to life.

2. Poetry Writing & Forms

Poetry uses rhythm, sound, and imaginative language to convey emotions and ideas concisely, often carrying deep meaning and expression.

3. Descriptive Writing Techniques

Descriptive writing uses sensory details to create vivid imagery. It helps readers imagine scenes, people, and experiences clearly.

4. Dialogue Writing

Dialogue reveals character, moves the story forward, and adds realism. Proper punctuation and natural speech make the conversation engaging and clear.

5. Narrative Writing

Narrative writing tells a story or shares an experience. Key elements include characters, setting, plot, and conflict, with a clear beginning, middle, and ending.
