Arts & Visual Design

Drawing, Painting, and Design Principles
Understand how points, lines, surfaces, and solids are formed and related.

Develop logical thinking, creativity, and problem-solving skills.

Lectures

1. Elements of Arts

Basic parts used to create artwork: line, shape, color, texture, form, space, and value. These elements are combined to express ideas and emotions effectively.

2. Principles of Design

Guidelines for arranging elements of art: balance, contrast, emphasis, movement, pattern, rhythm, and unity. They make artwork visually appealing and organized.

3. Color Theory

Study of how colors work together. Primary, secondary, and tertiary colors can create harmony or contrast, affect mood, and enhance designs.

4. Drawing and Illustration

Drawing uses tools like pencils or charcoal to create images. Illustration explains ideas visually. Practice improves accuracy, shading, and creativity.

5. Visual Communication

Using images to share ideas and messages via posters, logos, and digital designs. Effective visual communication is clear, engaging, and easy to understand.
